    1. Background

    Narcissus and the Machine explores various water forms through a machine learning model, translating these nuanced expressions into a captivating visual projection. To achieve this, the model is trained on different water motions, including rainfall, waterfalls, ocean waves, and water ripples. The process involves extracting frames from video footage of distinct water forms, followed by training a classifier model to discern and identify their intricate movement patterns as they manifest in the natural world. Once trained, the model interfaces with a webcam, dynamically analyzing real-time video footage of human body movements, classifying them as the individual forms of water. This interpolation is then visually expressed through an abstract graphical display.
    The ambition of this concept lies in the intention to illustrate how the natural world can be interpreted through the lens of machine learning. In doing so, the project aims to juxtapose the influence of digital technologies and generative AI with the enduring presence of our natural environment on Earth. Water, as our fundamental life force and reflective medium, serves as the focal point. The project draws inspiration from the myth of Narcissus, riffing on the title’s canonical fascination with appearance, reflection, and embodiment through water. In the age of generative AI, the project provocatively asks how our selves and bodies perpetually generate and recreate expressive forms in this evolving interplay between human creativity and technological innovation.
